Geography of Oman

Oman is a land of remarkable geographic diversity, featuring arid deserts, rugged mountains, fertile valleys, and pristine coastlines along the Arabian Sea. This variety shapes the climate, ecosystems, and lifestyle of its people.

Mountains & Highlands

The Hajar Mountains dominate northern Oman, with peaks like Jebel Shams and Jebel Akhdar. These highlands create dramatic landscapes of canyons, terraced farms, and wadis, supporting both wildlife and human settlements.

Deserts & Wadis

The central deserts, including Wahiba Sands, feature shifting dunes and unique ecosystems. Interspersed with these arid landscapes are lush wadis — valleys carved by seasonal rivers, supporting agriculture, wildlife, and small communities.
